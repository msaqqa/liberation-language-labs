Welcome to the labs! Liberation Language Labs was created in 2025 to be a place of constant experimentation, growth, and learning that centers the lived experiences, needs, and context of people who find themselves needing specialized speech therapy services. My practice strives to embody the 10 principles of Disability Justice, and answer the question: "What does it look like to center liberation and justice in the care we provide?"

I have been a practicing SLP since 2017, and have worked with people throughout the lifespan, in hospitals, daycare centers, nursing homes, and in pediatric/adult outpatient. Along the way, I developed specializations in articulation disorders, executive function, challenges, and social language skills. I view all communication as an ongoing experiment, and I deeply enjoy supporting my clients as they discover who they are, and become who they want to be.

A settler on unceded Lummi, Nooksack, and Semiahmoo lands (Bellingham) since 2002, I live with my partner and two cats. When not at work, I read, garden, mountainbike, and ski, and contribute to local and statewide campaigns for justice in Palestine. What is speech therapy?

structure meets child-led meets practical, real-world problem-solving

Assessments

We begin with a detailed evaluation, formally and informally assessing areas of concern and areas of strength. My assessments are holistic and consider the whole person, their frustrations as well as their goals. This also serves to determine if we are a good relational fit, and I encourage all my clients to feel comfortable asking for a referral if they don't want to pursue services with me. I may also refer out to another specialist, such as an occupational therapist, mental health counselor, or ENT, prior to recommending a therapy program.

Goal Setting

I strive to always make goals that are SMART: Specific, Measureable, Achievable, Relevant, and Timely. Therapy goals are determined collaboratively, and chosen so that they have the greatest positive impact on communication, growth, and personal agency.

Therapy programming

My goal is to ensure that everyone involved understands what we're working on, why we're working on it, and how we're working on it. Therapy sessions are 45 minutes long, one to three times per week, depending on scheduling availability.

Home Practice

My goal is to move through the therapy process as efficiently as possible, and so to that end, daily practice is an expected component of every therapy plan.

Progress Monitoring

Regular assessments are conducted to monitor progress. Based on progress, adjustments to the therapy plan are made. Ongoing feedback helps keep the therapy on track and ensures goals are being met.

Generalisation

The final goal is to ensure that improvements in speech and language skills are carried over into everyday life. This involves practicing communication in real-life situations outside of therapy sessions.

Services

I use a combination of structured and child-led treatment approaches, in the comfort of your own home, via telehealth, in your child's daycares, preschools, and private programs, when appropriate

My therapy services include:

  • Speech and Language Therapy
  • Standardized Evaluations
  • Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) Programming
  • Speech/Language/Fluency Screenings
  • School-Based Speech and Language Services
  • Family Counseling and Consultative Services

Diagnoses I treat include:

  • Expressive and/or receptive language delay/disorders
  • Articulation and/or phonological delay/disorders
  • Childhood Apraxia of Speech
  • Pragmatic language disorders (social skills)
  • Executive function disorders
  • Cognitive-communication disorders
  • Voice disorders
  • Paradoxical Vocal Fold Motion (PVFM)
