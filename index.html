What is speech therapy?

A structured, collaborative approach designed to support communication development from assessment to real-world application.

Assessment and Diagnosis

The process starts with a detailed evaluation by a speech-language pathologist (SLP). This professional assesses the individual’s speech, language, and communication skills. The assessment helps identify specific speech or language disorders, such as a swallow disorder, and forms the basis for a tailored treatment plan.

Goal Setting

Based on the assessment, specific, measurable goals are set. These goals target areas needing improvement, such as articulation, fluency, or language comprehension.

Therapy Sessions

Therapy sessions are conducted regularly, often weekly. Sessions involve various exercises and activities tailored to the individual’s needs. Activities might include practicing sound syllable, improving sentence structure, or enhancing language comprehension.

Home Practice

Patients are often given exercises to practice at home. Consistent practice is crucial for reinforcing skills learned during therapy sessions. Family involvement, particularly from a parent caregiver, can significantly enhance the effectiveness of therapy.

Progress Monitoring

Regular assessments are conducted to monitor progress. Based on progress, adjustments to the therapy plan are made. Ongoing feedback helps keep the therapy on track and ensures goals are being met.

Generalisation

The final goal is to ensure that improvements in speech and language skills are carried over into everyday life. This involves practicing communication in real-life situations outside of therapy sessions.

Jessica Burchiel — M.A. — CCC-SLP

I am a speech therapist who offers home health, telehealth, and in-school speech therapy sessions. A practicing SLP since 2017, I completed my fellowship year at Skagit Valley Hospital, treating dysphagia, cognitive and language disorders in adults on the medical and acute care floors.

In 2021 I made the decision to shift back to pediatrics, and began working at Catalyst therapies where I developed specializations in phonological disorders, executive function, and coaching social language skills for autistic clients.

I founded Liberation Language Labs in 2025 to be a place of constant experimentation, growth, and learning that centers the lived experiences, needs, and context of the children and families who need specialized speech therapy services. The practice strives to embody the 10 principles of Disability Justice, and answer the question: "What does it look like to center liberation and justice in the care we provide?"

As a resident of Bellingham since 2002, I live with my husband and two cats. When not at work, I read, garden, mountainbike, and ski, and contribute to local and statewide campaigns for justice in Palestine.

Services

I use a combination of structured and child-led treatment approaches, in the comfort of your own home, via telehealth, in your child's daycares, preschools, and private programs, when appropriate.

My therapy services include:

  • Speech and Language Therapy
  • Standardized Evaluations
  • Augmentative and Alternative Communication (AAC) Programming
  • Speech/Language/Fluency Screenings
  • School-Based Speech and Language Services
  • Family Counseling and Consultative Services

Diagnoses I treat include:

  • Expressive and/or receptive language delay/disorders
  • Articulation and/or phonological delay/disorders
  • Childhood Apraxia of Speech
  • Pragmatic language disorders (social skills)
  • Executive function disorders
  • Cognitive-communication disorders
  • Voice disorders
  • Paradoxical Vocal Fold Motion (PVFM)
